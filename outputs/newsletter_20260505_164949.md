## Five Eyes Agencies Issue Critical Warning on AI Agents
**Source:** aiagentstore.ai

The **Five Eyes** intelligence alliance, comprising the US, UK, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand, has issued a critical warning regarding the rapid deployment of agentic AI systems. These self-operating AI agents, while promising, pose significant risks if not managed carefully. The agencies recommend a cautious approach, advocating for the gradual integration of AI agents into workflows, starting with low-risk tasks and ensuring human oversight remains paramount.

This advisory comes as the landscape of AI technology evolves, with increased autonomy in AI systems raising concerns about potential malfunctions that could lead to severe consequences. The implications are clear: organizations must prioritize safety and control as they explore the capabilities of these advanced systems.

## Google Announces Free AI Agents Training
**Source:** aiagentstore.ai

In a bid to enhance understanding and skills in developing autonomous AI systems, **Google** has unveiled a five-day intensive training course focused on AI agents. Set to launch next month, the course is designed for individuals with basic Python knowledge and will cover various aspects of "agentic workflow" practices. While foundational materials will be available for free, advanced content may incur a fee.

This initiative underscores the growing importance of education in navigating the complexities of AI technology. Organizations looking to implement AI agents should consider this training as a valuable resource to better understand the potential and limitations of these systems.

## Salesforce Restructures as an Agent-First Platform
**Source:** aiagentstore.ai

**Salesforce** has officially transitioned to an agent-first platform with the introduction of **Headless 360**, which allows AI agents to access all workflows, objects, and business logic through APIs and command-line interfaces. This restructuring enables AI agents to operate with the same data access and permissions as human users, streamlining processes and enhancing productivity.

The shift signifies a broader trend in AI adoption, where the focus is moving from merely training models to optimizing their deployment and operational efficiency. As companies embrace this change, they must also address the challenges associated with scaling and managing these systems effectively.

## Haun Ventures Adds AI Agents to Its $1B Crypto Strategy

**Haun Ventures** has raised **$1 billion** to invest in crypto infrastructure, tokenization, and the development of AI agents. This significant funding round reflects a growing recognition of the potential for machine-led economic activity. The firm aims to leverage AI agents to enhance operational efficiencies within the crypto space.

This move highlights the increasing intersection between AI technology and cryptocurrency, as investors seek to capitalize on innovative solutions that can drive economic growth. As AI agents become more integrated into various sectors, their impact on market dynamics will be closely monitored.
